Phõ Bò

(Vietnamese Beef Noodle Soup)

This bowl of love is full of liquid and solids, which makes it such a great overall dish. It is hearty, lovely and most of all, full of a ton of beef (best part about it). To be honest, it takes way too long to make for a quick fix, but if you have the time, why not. This is a great dish for when you are hungry, not hungry, happy, sad, or when you have an existential life crisis. Honestly, just eat it. Unless if you are vegetarian, then you might not have a good time.

Ingredients

  • 6l water
  • 2 sweet onions
  • Ginger
  • 5lbs beef bone
  • Spices:
    • 5-10 Star anise
    • 10 cloves
    • 2 cinnamon sticks
    • 3 tbsp black pepper
    • 5 cardamompods
  • Beef brisket
  • ¼ cup Fish sauce
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • Rice noodles (cook by packaged instructions)
  • Beef tenderloin
  • Beef balls
  • Beef tripe
  • Vietnamese basil
  • Beansprouts

Instructions

  1. Roast spices and sweet onion until browned. This will take 10-20 minutes.
  2. Bring water to boil in a large pot.
  3. Place in beef bones and allow to simmer for 1 hour.
  4. Take out beef bones and rinse. Place the bones back into the simmering water.
  5. Put in beef brisket, spices and ginger into pot. Allow to simmer for 8-10 hours.
  6. Add in fish sauce and sugar.
  7. Take out beef brisket to cut and serve with dish.
  8. When serving, prepare bowl with cooked rice noodles, slices of beef brisket, raw beef, beef balls, and beef tripe.
  9. Garnish with Vietnamese basil and beansprouts.
